Pope John Paul II

Here is a time line of Pope John Paul II

  • May 18, 1920: Karol Jozef Wojtyla is born in Wadowice, Poland.
  • 1937-1939: When the Nazis invade Poland in September 1939, moves underground.
  • Nov. 1, 1946: Ordained as a priest.
  • June 28, 1967: Consecrated as a cardinal by Pope Paul VI.
  • Oct. 16, 1978: Elected successor to Pope John Paul I, becoming the 264th pope of the Catholic Church, the first Polish pope ever!
  • June 2, 1979: Visits Poland for the first time as pope, Causes a uprising that turns into the massive Solidarność movement.
